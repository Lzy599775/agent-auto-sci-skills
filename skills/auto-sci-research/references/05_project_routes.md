## 3. Conceptual Boundary

- Accessibility is the measurement layer: can people reach facilities, how long does it take, and by which mode?
- Equity is the distribution layer: are opportunities uneven across groups and places?
- Spatial justice is the normative and governance layer: are inequalities embedded in procedures, recognition, and institutional design?

Keep these three layers explicit in every manuscript route.

## 4. Analysis Sections

1. Performance analysis: annual output, countries, institutions, authors, journals, citations.
2. Collaboration structure: country, institution, author networks; ordinary network plus chord diagram.
3. Intellectual structure: co-cited references, co-cited journals, burst references.
4. Conceptual structure: author keyword co-occurrence, Louvain clusters, thematic map, temporal evolution.
5. Methodological evolution: method-family coding, trends, heatmaps, stacked plots.
6. Justice framing: distributional, procedural, recognitional justice trends and method links.
7. High-impact subset: top 10% high-impact articles and their topics, methods, theory bases.
8. Planning agenda: object, method, vulnerable groups, governance mechanism, planning intervention.
