# Workflow Checklists

## Full Writing Pipeline

1. Intake: target journal, study object, analysis unit, data availability, contribution type, figure and table set.
2. Argument blueprint: public problem, research gap, framework, research questions, claim-evidence map.
3. Data and method audit: every variable has a purpose, every formula has units and parameters, every model has validation, every spatial unit is justified.
4. Draft: write Introduction after the claim-evidence map; write Data/Methods as reproducibility contract; write Results with claim-led openings; write Discussion as mechanism and planning translation.
5. Figure/table pass: each figure proves one claim; comparable maps are visually comparable; table values are needed for verification; captions define units, data, and panels.
6. Journal adaptation: read the target journal playbook and reweight the story.
7. Final review: Introduction and Conclusion answer the same questions; Results do not overclaim causality; Discussion does not repeat Results; limitations are specific.

## Research Question Test

Good research questions ask about pattern, equity, and mechanism; match available data; produce figure/table evidence; and support a planning or theoretical implication.

Weak questions only ask what the distribution is, mention a model but not a problem, cannot be answered by current data, or have no policy/theoretical consequence.

## Pre-Submission Reviewer Simulation

1. Why is this a sport geography problem rather than a generic accessibility study?
2. Why does the study area matter?
3. How are sports facility capacity and quality represented?
4. Are age or vulnerable groups treated as need-sensitive demand?
5. Does the model distinguish access, exposure, use, and benefit?
6. Are green and heat variables interpreted as mechanisms or just controls?
7. Is SHAP or ML validated and translated into planning language?
8. Are color scales and map classifications defensible?
9. Is each policy recommendation directly traceable to a result?
10. Would the same conclusion hold under another threshold, decay function, or spatial unit?
