# Journal Playbooks

These are journal-specific subskills. Read the target section before drafting or revising for that journal.

## Nature Communications / npj Urban Sustainability

### Fit

Use when the study has large scale, long time series, cross-city comparison, conceptual novelty, or a compact but powerful evidence chain.

### Manuscript logic

`global/large-scale challenge -> overlooked exposure or equity dimension -> harmonized data and metric -> broad pattern -> driver attribution -> implications for urban sustainability`

### Writing style

Write a continuous narrative. Keep the number of claims small but strongly supported. Avoid local policy details dominating the main story.

### Figures

4 to 6 main figures. Typical sequence: global/cross-city map, trend, inequality, drivers, case examples. Captions must be self-contained.

### Red flags

Single-city evidence without broader conceptual contribution; too many technical results in main text.

## Sustainable Cities and Society

### Fit

Use for sustainability, climate resilience, heat-green-sport coupling, active-health infrastructure, and planning-translatable method chains.

### Manuscript logic

`urban sustainability pressure -> resource opportunity or risk -> refined metric -> spatial/equity pattern -> drivers -> sustainable planning strategies`

### Writing style

Methods can be detailed if they form a coherent framework. Introduction should connect to sustainability, health, resilience, or low-carbon planning. Discussion should translate findings into sustainable city or community planning.

### Figures

Study area, workflow, spatial pattern, model explanation, and strategy map. Heat/green maps must use consistent palettes. SHAP or optimization figures should be tied to planning decisions.

### Best angle for this project

Position sports facilities as part of sustainable active-health infrastructure whose usability is constrained by green and heat exposure.

## Cities / Applied Geography / Habitat International

### Fit

Use for urban governance, public service equity, accessibility, social group differences, center-periphery mismatch, and planning policy.

### Manuscript logic

`public service fairness -> accessibility measurement gap -> spatial and social inequity -> mechanism -> governance implication`

### Writing style

Make the city problem visible early. Results should identify where, who, and why. Discussion should produce planning typologies.

### Figures

Study area and facility distribution, accessibility maps, group comparison and Gini/Lorenz, driver or spatial-regression results, strategy typology.

### Best angle for this project

This is the strongest default route for sport facility accessibility and sport park equity papers.

## Landscape and Urban Planning / Urban Forestry & Urban Greening

### Fit

Use when the paper emphasizes park quality, green infrastructure, landscape pattern, ecological services, or design/management.

### Manuscript logic

`urban landscape resource -> quality/access/use issue -> refined measurement -> service or wellbeing implication -> design/management strategy`

### Writing style

Go beyond where facilities are to what service quality they provide. Include green quantity, quality, shade, cooling, activity diversity, or maintenance if available.

### Figures

Use study area overview, typical site zoom-ins, landscape pattern maps, accessibility/exposure maps, and strategy diagrams. Natural palettes are acceptable, but avoid an all-green manuscript.

### Best angle for this project

Use this route when sports parks are framed as compound green-active spaces.

## Ecological Indicators / Environment International / Environmental Research / Social Science & Medicine

### Fit

Use for indicator construction, exposure measurement, health pathways, systematic reviews, environmental justice, and robust quantitative reporting.

### Manuscript logic

`measurement problem -> indicator/framework -> validation -> exposure/equity/health association -> reporting standard or intervention implication`

### Writing style

Define every indicator carefully. Report formulas, units, normalization, weights, and sensitivity. For health outcomes, distinguish association, prediction, and causation.

### Figures

Indicator framework, exposure-response or stratified effect plots, sensitivity analysis, evidence path or PRISMA if review-based.

### Best angle for this project

Use this route for a green-heat-sport exposure index or an active-health opportunity index.

## Chinese Planning / Sport Journals

### Fit

Use for local planning practice, policy implementation, sports facility configuration, life-circle planning, and governance recommendations.

### Manuscript logic

`policy demand -> current spatial problem -> evaluation framework -> zoning diagnosis -> planning strategy`

### Writing style

Connect to Healthy China, National Fitness, sports park construction, 15-minute community life circle, and territorial spatial planning. Results should be easy for planning departments to use.

### Figures

Strategy maps and zoning maps are important. Use clearer labels and practical categories. Keep formulas concise and move technical details to appendix if needed.

### Best angle for this project

Translate SCI-style evidence into planning language: fill gaps, improve quality, strengthen connections, retrofit heat/green constraints, and prioritize vulnerable groups.
