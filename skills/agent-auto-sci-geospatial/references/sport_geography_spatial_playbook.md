# Sport Geography Spatial Playbook

Use this reference for sport parks, sport facilities, green exposure, heat exposure, urban health, and equity analysis.

## 1. Concept Boundaries

Do not collapse these concepts:

| Concept | Meaning | Typical metric |
|---|---|---|
| availability | whether resources exist | count, area, facility type |
| accessibility | effort to reach resources | network time, 2SFCA, gravity access |
| exposure | environmental condition around people/places | NDVI, LST, park area, canopy, LCZ |
| quality | condition or usability of resource | amenities, maintenance, sport type diversity |
| use | actual behavior | visits, activity records, surveys |
| equity | distribution relative to need and vulnerability | gap index, concentration, priority areas |

## 2. Sport Park And Facility Metrics

| Research angle | Useful metrics |
|---|---|
| supply | number, area, type diversity, capacity proxy |
| proximity | nearest distance, walking time, catchment coverage |
| network accessibility | cumulative opportunity, gravity, 2SFCA, route impedance |
| green co-benefit | NDVI/canopy/park size near sport facility |
| heat burden | LST, LCZ, shade proxy, impervious surface |
| equity | access by age, income, migrants, school-age youth, older adults |
| policy priority | low access + high vulnerability + high heat/low green |

## 3. Green And Heat Exposure

Recommended separation:

- green exposure: amount and quality of vegetation or parks;
- sport opportunity: active-living infrastructure;
- heat exposure: thermal stress and shade deficit;
- combined health opportunity: green + sport + low heat + reachable by network.

Avoid saying "green exposure improves sport participation" unless use or behavior evidence is available.

## 4. Sensitivity Checks

Run at least one relevant sensitivity set:

- buffer radius: 300/500/800/1000 m;
- network threshold: 5/10/15/20 minutes;
- travel mode: walking vs cycling vs public transport;
- facility inclusion: all sport facilities vs public-only vs quality-filtered;
- exposure aggregation: mean, median, percentile, threshold share;
- spatial unit: community, census tract, grid, district.

## 5. China Map Compliance

For publication figures involving China:

- use standard map service or official compliant base material;
- preserve national boundary, South China Sea inset/nine-dash line where required;
- do not crop official boundaries in a misleading way;
- keep audit/source note in project files;
- ensure scale, north arrow, and boundary hierarchy are clear.

## 6. Policy Interpretation Ladder

Move from map to policy in steps:

1. describe spatial pattern;
2. identify underserved or overburdened groups;
3. test sensitivity to metric choices;
4. link pattern to plausible mechanism;
5. state intervention lever;
6. note implementation data needed;
7. avoid causal or behavioral claims unless design supports them.

## 7. Reviewer Risks

Common reviewer objections:

- accessibility metric ignores facility quality or capacity;
- green space is treated as sport facility;
- exposure date does not match population or facility data;
- random ML split ignores spatial leakage;
- map lacks compliance or source transparency;
- equity claim lacks vulnerability or need denominator;
- policy recommendation is generic and not linked to evidence.
