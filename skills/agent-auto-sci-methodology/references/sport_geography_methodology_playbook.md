# Sport Geography Methodology Playbook

Use this reference to strengthen study design for sport parks, sport facilities, green exposure, heat exposure, parks, urban health, and equity.

## 1. Core Concept Framework

A defensible framework separates:

`urban context -> environmental exposure -> sport opportunity/accessibility -> population vulnerability -> behavior or health pathway -> planning response`

Do not skip directly from green-space area to health outcomes unless the causal bridge is supported.

## 2. Main Research Routes

| Route | Best question | Needed evidence |
|---|---|---|
| review/bibliometric | what does the field know, miss, and overclaim? | search strategy, coding framework, critical synthesis |
| spatial equity | who has less access relative to need? | accessibility metric, population/vulnerability denominator |
| exposure interaction | how do green/heat/sport opportunity overlap? | harmonized spatial units and exposure metrics |
| urban-health mechanism | how might environments shape activity/health? | behavior/health data or careful mechanism boundary |
| policy agenda | what should planners prioritize? | evidence map, implementation lever, uncertainty |

## 3. Bias And Validity Risks

| Risk | Example | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| MAUP | results change by grid/community/district | sensitivity by spatial unit |
| spatial confounding | wealthier areas have both more parks and better health | covariates, stratification, cautious interpretation |
| selection bias | active people choose greener areas | avoid causal overclaim or use stronger design |
| exposure misclassification | NDVI date differs from population/facility date | temporal audit and sensitivity |
| facility-quality omission | count treats poor and high-quality facilities equally | quality proxy or limitation |
| potential-access gap | network access is not actual use | precise wording and behavior-data caveat |
| publication/corpus bias | WoS English literature excludes local evidence | database boundary and sensitivity |

## 4. Systematic Review Coding Dimensions

For review manuscripts, code:

- exposure/accessibility construct;
- sport facility or park type;
- spatial unit and scale;
- population group;
- outcome or policy domain;
- method type;
- data source;
- equity dimension;
- health/behavior mechanism;
- evidence strength;
- policy recommendation type;
- limitations.

## 5. Mechanism Testing Ladder

| Claim strength | Required support |
|---|---|
| descriptive pattern | map/table and clear metric |
| inequity | distribution relative to need or vulnerability |
| association | statistical model and robustness |
| mechanism suggestion | association plus literature and plausible pathway |
| causal effect | longitudinal/quasi-experimental/experimental identification |
| policy priority | evidence, affected population, lever, feasibility, risk |

## 6. Target-Journal Method Expectations

| Journal route | Method emphasis |
|---|---|
| Cities | urban policy contribution, governance relevance, spatial inequality |
| Sustainable Cities and Society | sustainability systems, environment-health-planning linkage |
| UFUG | green-space quality/function and urban greening evidence |
| Environment International | exposure assessment, health relevance, bias control |
| Nature Cities | concise, conceptually strong, transferable urban insight |

## 7. Pre-Submission Method Audit

Before drafting claims:

- Are the constructs and metrics aligned?
- Are inclusion/exclusion decisions reproducible?
- Are spatial and temporal scales justified?
- Are weak evidence areas explicitly retained?
- Are limitations specific rather than generic?
- Does the policy agenda follow from evidence rather than from preference?
