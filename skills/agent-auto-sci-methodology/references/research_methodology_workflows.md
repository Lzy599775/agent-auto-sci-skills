# Research Methodology Workflows

## 1. Research Question Builder

Every research question should specify:

- object;
- population or spatial unit;
- exposure or intervention;
- outcome;
- method;
- comparison;
- spatial and temporal scope;
- evidence needed.

## 2. Hypothesis Matrix

| Hypothesis | Mechanism | Observable evidence | Test | Falsifier | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Always include at least one competing explanation.

## 3. Evidence Quality Audit

Check:

- internal validity;
- external validity;
- construct validity;
- statistical conclusion validity;
- measurement quality;
- confounding;
- selection bias;
- temporal ambiguity;
- publication or language bias.

## 4. Spatial Justice Framework

Use four dimensions:

- distributional justice: where resources and harms are located;
- procedural justice: who participates in decisions;
- recognitional justice: whose needs and identities are recognized;
- capability justice: whether formal access becomes real opportunity.

## 5. Claim Strength

| Evidence | Suggested language |
|---|---|
| direct causal evidence | suggests that X contributes to Y |
| robust observational association | is associated with |
| predictive model attribution | is a strong predictor in the model |
| bibliometric co-occurrence | indicates a research linkage |
| theory only | may provide a possible explanation |
