# Positioning And Journal Route

Use this reference when choosing topic scope, target journal, title, contribution, and article type.

## 1. Manuscript Types

Choose one primary route.

### Systematic Review

Use when the question is evidence-oriented:

- What is known about urban sport/green/open-space exposure and health or equity?
- Which methods are used to measure accessibility, exposure, or justice?
- Which population groups are covered or neglected?

Expected features:

- reproducible databases and search strings;
- PRISMA flow;
- inclusion/exclusion criteria;
- quality or evidence appraisal when health claims are central;
- thematic synthesis rather than paper-by-paper summary.

### Scoping Review

Use when the field is fragmented or emerging:

- sports facilities + green space + active recreation infrastructure;
- heat exposure + cooling access + green equity;
- spatial justice framing in urban parks and active living spaces.

Expected features:

- broad concept map;
- taxonomy of objects, methods, scales, populations, and justice dimensions;
- future research agenda.

### Bibliometric / Scientometric Review

Use when the question is about field evolution:

- publication trends, collaboration networks, intellectual bases, keyword evolution, burst references;
- method-family evolution and topic frontiers.

Expected features:

- database clarity, de-duplication, thesaurus;
- performance analysis, collaboration, intellectual structure, conceptual structure;
- manual interpretation that turns maps into arguments.

### Hybrid Review

Use for SCS/Cities-level submissions when bibliometrics alone is too descriptive.

Recommended structure:

`bibliometric mapping + systematic/scoping screening + manual critical coding + planning agenda`

This is the default route for the user's current project.

## 2. Topic Scope

Avoid a narrow title if the corpus is broader than sports parks.

Prefer:

- urban active recreation infrastructure;
- urban green/open/sport space exposure;
- public physical activity resources;
- sport and green space opportunity;
- exposure inequality in urban recreation environments.

Use "sports parks" only when the corpus is truly dominated by sports parks.

## 3. Contribution Types

State contribution as one or two of:

- concept boundary: clarifying sport facility, recreation facility, green space, park, playground, open space, and active recreation infrastructure;
- metric framework: accessibility, availability, proximity, exposure, use, quality, and cooling service;
- method evolution: buffers, network distance, 2SFCA, gravity, SDNA, mobile phone data, social media, remote sensing, ML/GeoAI;
- justice framework: distributional, procedural, recognition, intergenerational, climate, and health justice;
- policy agenda: planning standards, priority areas, vulnerable groups, quality upgrading, heat adaptation, 15-minute city, and participatory governance.

## 4. Journal Routes

### Sustainable Cities and Society

Best fit when the manuscript links:

- sustainable cities;
- healthy built environments;
- urban resilience or heat adaptation;
- green infrastructure;
- decision support;
- equity-aware planning methods.

Write as a method-chain and policy-agenda paper:

`problem -> data/method evolution -> equity/health implication -> sustainable planning agenda`

Avoid a pure bibliometric paper unless it produces a new framework or agenda.

### Cities

Best fit when the manuscript links:

- urban governance and planning;
- spatial justice;
- public service allocation;
- policy and management implications;
- city-scale or neighbourhood-scale urban processes.

Write as a planning and urban studies paper:

`urban problem -> uneven opportunity -> governance/mechanism gap -> planning agenda`

Avoid technical GIS/remote sensing emphasis without clear planning application.

### Landscape And Urban Planning / Urban Forestry & Urban Greening

Best fit when the manuscript focuses on:

- green space quality;
- park planning;
- landscape services;
- biodiversity/health/co-benefits;
- user experience and landscape design.

Emphasise landscape configuration, quality, service provision, and evidence for design decisions.

### Health & Place / Environment International / Environmental Research

Best fit when the manuscript focuses on:

- health pathways;
- exposure assessment;
- vulnerable populations;
- environmental epidemiology;
- robust evidence synthesis.

Do not overclaim causality unless evidence quality supports it.

## 5. Title Patterns

Use one of these patterns:

- `Exposure inequality in urban active recreation infrastructure: A systematic scientometric review`
- `From accessibility to spatial justice: A critical review of urban green and sport space equity`
- `Urban green and sport space exposure for healthy cities: Evidence, methods, and planning agendas`
- `Measuring equitable access to active living environments: A bibliometric and critical review`
