# Manuscript Structure

Use this reference when drafting the review or bibliometric paper.

## 1. Abstract

Use a six-move abstract:

1. public problem;
2. field fragmentation;
3. databases and methods;
4. main knowledge trends;
5. critical gaps;
6. planning/policy agenda.

Avoid saying only "we used CiteSpace and VOSviewer".

## 2. Introduction

Recommended chain:

1. active living, urban health, climate adaptation, and equitable public-space provision are urgent urban issues;
2. sports facilities, parks, playgrounds, and green/open spaces are opportunity infrastructures;
3. existing studies measure accessibility, exposure, quality, and use with inconsistent methods;
4. equity studies often remain distributional and underdevelop procedural/recognition justice;
5. bibliometric mapping alone cannot reveal these conceptual and planning gaps;
6. this review combines scientometric analysis, systematic screening, and critical coding.

End with research questions:

- How has the field evolved?
- Which methods and metrics dominate?
- How are equity and justice framed?
- Which populations and planning implications are underdeveloped?
- What agenda should guide future sport geography and healthy-city research?

## 3. Methods

Include:

- review type;
- databases;
- search string;
- dates and filters;
- de-duplication;
- PRISMA screening;
- bibliometric software and parameters;
- thesaurus construction;
- manual coding framework;
- inter-coder or audit procedure if applicable;
- limitations of database coverage.

## 4. Results

Recommended order:

1. corpus profile and publication growth;
2. geographic, source, and disciplinary distribution;
3. intellectual bases and landmark papers;
4. conceptual clusters and thematic evolution;
5. method-family evolution;
6. justice/population coding;
7. high-impact or target-journal subset.

## 5. Discussion

Use four layers:

1. What the field has learned;
2. What current metrics still miss;
3. How justice and vulnerable groups should be reframed;
4. How planning should respond.

For Cities, emphasise governance and planning.

For Sustainable Cities and Society, emphasise sustainability, healthy environments, heat/resilience, decision support, and policy translation.

## 6. Conclusion

Answer the research questions directly.

Do not introduce new results. State:

- conceptual contribution;
- methodological contribution;
- practical agenda;
- boundary conditions.
