# K-Dense Scientific Communication Mapping

Use this reference when adapting K-Dense scientific writing, peer review, slides, schematics, and citation-management skills.

## 1. Included Skill Families

| K-Dense skill family | Local use | Domain adaptation |
|---|---|---|
| scientific writing | manuscript structure, paragraph logic, claim-evidence alignment | Cities/SCS/UFUG/EI/Nature-family review and empirical writing |
| peer review | internal critique, risk finding, revision prioritization | reviewer-risk scan before submission |
| scientific slides | academic presentation and defense | conference talk, thesis group meeting, method/result storytelling |
| scientific schematics | conceptual framework and workflow diagram | exposure-accessibility-health mechanism, policy agenda |
| citation management | citation audit and bibliography hygiene | WoS/Scopus/Zotero/Mendeley/manual non-OA corpus |

## 2. Writing Workflow

1. Define target journal and article type.
2. Build claim-evidence map before drafting.
3. Select manuscript architecture: empirical IMRAD, systematic review, scoping review, bibliometric review, or critical agenda.
4. Plan figures/tables before writing the Results or synthesis.
5. Draft around claims, not around tool outputs.
6. Run internal review for novelty, evidence, method, writing, and policy overclaim.
7. Convert critique into revisions and response notes.

## 3. Claim-Evidence Map

Minimum fields:

| field | meaning |
|---|---|
| `claim_id` | stable ID |
| `claim` | exact statement to support |
| `evidence` | data, figure, table, cited literature |
| `strength` | strong, moderate, weak |
| `boundary` | scope and limitation |
| `journal_value` | why the target journal should care |
| `reviewer_risk` | likely objection |

## 4. Peer-Review Scan

Before submission, scan:

- Is the problem international enough for the target journal?
- Does the method answer the stated question?
- Are causal words controlled?
- Does every figure support a necessary claim?
- Are policy recommendations specific and evidence-bounded?
- Are limitations honest but not self-defeating?
- Are citations current, relevant, and not invented?

## 5. Rebuttal Workflow

For reviewer comments:

1. split each comment into atomic issues;
2. classify as conceptual, methodological, data, writing, citation, or presentation;
3. decide accept, partially accept, clarify, or respectfully disagree;
4. identify manuscript change and exact location;
5. write response with thanks, action, evidence, and boundary;
6. avoid defensive tone and vague promises.

## 6. Slide/Poster Rules

- One message per slide or poster panel.
- Method diagrams should reduce cognitive load, not reproduce code.
- Results slides should show the figure first, then concise interpretation.
- Policy slides should separate evidence from recommendation.
- Use consistent colors for exposure, accessibility, vulnerability, and policy priority.

## 7. Citation Rules

- Do not invent article titles, DOIs, pages, or journal policies.
- Separate downloaded non-OA PDFs from public metadata.
- Keep search date, database, query, and inclusion status.
- Use citation managers or structured tables for final bibliography checks.
