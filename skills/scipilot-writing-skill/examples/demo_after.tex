Deep learning has advanced quickly, yet sample efficiency under domain shift remains an open problem. We address it with a lightweight adapter: a frozen backbone feeds a task-specific routing layer that is trained on a fraction of the usual data. Across four benchmarks, the method reaches 95\% accuracy, a gain of 3.1 points over the strongest baseline. The margin is stable. It holds whether labels are scarce or abundant, which suggests the gain does not hinge on data volume. Why it works, we cannot yet say.
